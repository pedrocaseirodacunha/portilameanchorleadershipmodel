Leading Through Change

How can holistic leadership be realistically applied
to help organizations lead effectively
in times of constant change?
Alejandra Matallana · Devin Davis · Dominik Wohr · Pedro Cunha · Teresa Torres
IE Business School × Porto Business School — March 2026
Discover

What works when leadership is at its best?

Across five organizations and five countries, we found the same five truths.

01

Trust comes first

The best leaders earned trust by extending it. No micromanagement, no surveillance — genuine belief in people's ability to deliver.

02

Flexibility unlocks potential

Autonomy during disruption made teams stronger. People given space to adapt found better solutions than those given strict instructions.

03

Open communication

Leaders who shared optimism, acknowledged effort, and accepted imperfection built the strongest teams. Transparency over perfection.

04

Proactive support

The best structures were built in, not deployed in crisis mode. Support systems that existed before they were needed made all the difference.

05

Strengths over problems

The best leaders focused on what people do well and built around that. They created conditions to amplify strengths — not fix weaknesses. Success followed naturally.

The dream is simple

Organizations where people thrive — not just survive. Where leaders focus on amplifying each person's strengths, creating conditions for success. Where change is met with resilience, not fear. Where performance is sustained by building on what makes people strong.

Design

Six practices for leading through change

Grounded in what we learned across five days of research, science, and group reflection.

Self-awareness

Know your strengths, biases, and energy patterns. Leading from your best self makes others follow with theirs.

Trust-building narrative

Share the real story — values, purpose, vulnerability. People follow leaders they believe, not leaders who perform.

Healthy rhythms

Protect energy through cadence: meeting rhythms, recovery time, decision-free zones. Respect biological clocks — not just business clocks.

Psychological safety

Create space for mistakes, questions, and honest feedback. Belonging is not comfort — it is the safety to be real.

Growth through strengths

Focus on each person's strengths and create conditions to amplify them. Growth happens when people own outcomes built on what they do best.

Mindful resilience

Build reflection into daily leadership. Pause before reacting. Seek the blind spots. Choose presence over productivity theatre.

Destiny

From insight to action

What can realistically be implemented — starting now.

First 30 days

Listen & Diagnose

Run a simple well-being pulse. Start from strengths — identify where trust, energy, and safety are already strong, then grow from there.

First 90 days

Introduce Practices

Start small: weekly reflection rituals, peer recognition, protected recovery time. Build habits before building systems.

First year

Embed & Evolve

Integrate practices into culture. Measure impact through engagement and energy — not just performance. Adjust based on real feedback.

"The best time to plant a tree was twenty years ago. The second best time is now."

The commitment is not perfection — it is starting from what is already strong.
