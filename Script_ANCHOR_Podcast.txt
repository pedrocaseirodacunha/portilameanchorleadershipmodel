ANCHOR FRAMEWORK — PODCAST NARRATION SCRIPT
Full narrative about the entire process: context, difficulties, losses, gains, framework, tools, and lessons
Group 3 | IE Business School × Porto Business School | March 2026
Voice: American English, natural but not rushed, conversational and reflective

---

Welcome to the ANCHOR Framework podcast. This is the story of how a real company's crisis became a framework for holistic leadership.

---

Let's start with the company. PORTILAME. Portuguese wood construction. Around one hundred people. One hundred families. Growing at forty percent per year for the last several years. Twenty years of history, built on culture, trust, and craftsmanship.

That sounds like success, right? It was. But success at that speed comes with a cost nobody warns you about.

---

Rapid growth broke something. The founders realized they no longer had the capacity or knowledge to lead what they had built. Too many decisions depended on too few people. The culture that held everything together — the thing that made people proud to come to work — started to fracture.

This is the part nobody talks about in business school. Growth can be the thing that kills what made you special.

---

And PORTILAME is not alone. This is a common story. Sixty-six percent of European workers have experienced burnout. Seventy percent of change management initiatives fail. Only thirteen percent of employees in Europe are truly engaged at work.

The world is changing faster than organizations can adapt. The skills people have today — thirty-nine percent of them will be disrupted by 2030. Portugal leads the European Union in burnout risk. Seventy-five percent of sick leave cost in Spain is mental health related.

These are not abstract statistics. These are people. Families. Children who notice when their parents come home drained instead of proud.

---

So we asked a different question. Not "what's broken?" But "when is leadership at its best?"

This is the core of Appreciative Inquiry — the methodology that anchored our entire week at IE Business School. Instead of diagnosing problems, you discover strengths. Instead of fixing what's wrong, you amplify what works.

Across five organizations, from five different countries, the same five truths kept coming back.

Trust comes first. Always. Given before it is earned. Not as policy, but as daily practice.

Flexibility unlocks people. When you give autonomy, people find better solutions than any manual could provide.

Open communication means being honest, not performing confidence. Sharing optimism while acknowledging imperfection.

Support structures are built before crisis, not during. The organizations that thrive have support already baked in.

And shared purpose reduces uncertainty. When people understand the why, they figure out the how.

At PORTILAME, this was the DNA from day one. As they say: "Who likes, comes. Who loves, stays."

---

Then came the dream. And this is the moment that changed everything for us.

A child of an employee said something at a company event that stopped the room.

"My hero is my father. When I grow up, I want to be like him. I want to work at PORTILAME."

That is the measure of a truly healthy organization. Not metrics. Not awards. Not efficiency ratios. Children who see their parents come home proud and fulfilled.

PORTILAME had this. They built it organically over twenty years. Then rapid growth fractured it. And the founders — to their credit — had the humility to acknowledge it and start rebuilding.

The mission became clear: recover this culture, protect it, systematize it. And from that journey, build something any organization can use. A framework so light that people carry it like identity — not obligation.

---

From this real organizational pain, we built the ANCHOR framework. Six dimensions. Three games. One anchor.

A, for Awareness. Know yourself. Your strengths through character science. Your biases through metacognition. Your attention limits — the average human focus span is only forty-four seconds now. Leaders who don't understand their own patterns cannot lead others through change.

N, for Narrative. Tell the real story. This isn't motivational speaking. Neuroscience proves that authentic storytelling changes brain chemistry. Oxytocin goes up. Cortisol goes down. The narrative that PORTILAME built — "Being PORTILAME" — is not a slogan. It is the story that held a culture together.

C, for Cadence. Protect your rhythms. Circadian science from Day 4 showed us that the brain thrives on predictable energy cycles and suffers from chaotic ones. Blue light suppresses melatonin by seventy percent with just one hour of evening exposure. Weekly rituals. Monthly recognition. Quarterly reviews. Digital discipline. These are not nice-to-haves. They are biological necessities.

H, for Humanity. Create belonging. Psychological safety — Day 2 with Laura Maguire — showed us that it has three dimensions: permission to make mistakes, genuine acknowledgement, and a sense of belonging. Without safety, there is no honesty. Without honesty, there is no trust.

O, for Organization. Build structures that free people, not trap them. Governance that enables, not constrains. Roles clear enough that autonomy feels safe. This is where growth meets structure — and where most organizations get it wrong.

R, for Resilience. Protect your energy. Day 4 with Daniel de la Serna proved it biologically: metabolic flexibility, mitochondrial health, and physical activity are not wellness luxuries. They are leadership infrastructure. Too much comfort actually degrades resilience. Recovery is not weakness. It is strategy.

---

Then came the Cisco case study, and it validated everything.

Mario Sebastián, Vice President of Customer Experience for Cisco in Europe. A billion-dollar operation. One hundred and eighty people. Two thousand clients.

When his division faced declining business, reorganizations, layoffs, fear, and disengagement, his instinct was exactly what most leaders do: fix the problem. More control. More meetings. Focus on issues.

The result? Things got worse.

Then one question changed everything. "Tell me about a time things worked really well." The energy in the room shifted immediately. People stopped defending and started remembering.

Three discoveries emerged. Focus shapes behavior — what you pay attention to grows. Energy drives performance — not compliance. And fixing does not equal improving. Solving problems returns you to baseline. Building on strengths takes you beyond it.

Cisco built a strengths-based organization using CliftonStrengths, coaching, weekly check-ins focused on what people do best, and the same Appreciative Inquiry cycle we used. The leader's role shifted from decision maker to context creator — someone who creates conditions for others to excel and grow.

This is not theory. This is a global enterprise proving that the principles behind ANCHOR work at scale.

---

To measure progress, we designed a calibration engine with three layers.

The Radar Score measures internal health. How healthy is each ANCHOR dimension right now? Self-assessment plus team pulse surveys. This is your internal truth.

The Universal KPI benchmarks against the best in the industry. Where do you stand compared to best-in-class organizations? This is your external reality check.

The Galactic KPI sets visionary targets for three to five years out. Where should each dimension be? This is your ambition. Aspirational, predictive, driving growth beyond what the market does today.

You are never finished. You are always recalibrating. The framework evolves with you.

---

But a framework without practice is just theory. And theory is exactly what organizations don't need more of.

So we created six tools. Not bureaucratic processes. Organic rituals that people actually want to do.

Peer Pulse Check. Two minutes a week. One strength observed, one growth area. No hierarchy, no forms.

Recognition Rituals. Monthly spotlight celebrating who lived the values. Who showed Convergence, Know-how, Progress, Empathy, or Sustainability this month?

Cadence Reviews. Weekly rhythm checks. Are our schedules sustainable? Is our energy being protected or depleted?

Appreciative Problem-Solving. When a problem appears, instead of jumping to fix, ask: when have we solved something similar well? What made that work? What would the best version look like?

Initiative Ownership. Each person picks one improvement per quarter. Not assigned by management — chosen by the individual. Everyone is a leader from their position.

And Weekly Reflection. Fifteen minutes at the end of each week. What went well? What did I learn? What will I change? This builds the metacognitive capacity that María José Ferrari taught us on Day 3.

---

Before implementing any of this, organizations must diagnose first. We defined six diagnostic rules.

DNA Clarity — are the founding values still alive and actionable?

Growth versus Structure — has the organization outgrown its governance model?

Leadership Bottleneck — how many critical decisions still require the founder?

Cultural Transmission — can a new employee learn the culture without the founders explaining it?

Knowledge Institutionalization — is intelligence in systems, or trapped in people's heads?

People Investment — is human development proportional to equipment investment?

These rules come from PORTILAME's own experience. They are not academic exercises. They are hard-won lessons.

---

Then the roadmap unfolds.

Thirty days: listen. Map strengths. Set the baseline. Start the first narrative act.

Ninety days: install cadence. Build psychological safety. Clarify governance. Benchmark against the industry.

Twelve months: full deployment. Galactic KPI targets. Succession depth. Annual recalibration.

This is not a project with an end date. It is a permanent operating system for holistic leadership.

---

And here is what makes this real, not theoretical: PORTILAME already lives much of this.

External sports funding across athletics, football, cycling, and more. Internal employee sports programmes with branded equipment — "EU SOU PORTILAME," which literally means "I am PORTILAME" — people wearing the company identity in personal competitions.

Partnerships with local sports facilities for weekly access. The Call Semanal — a weekly thirty-minute knowledge-sharing session where any employee can present. The annual Kick-Off that reconnects everyone to the company's DNA.

Structural equity in roles and compensation. Community engagement with universities, NGOs, and artists.

These are not plans on a roadmap. They are operational realities. The ANCHOR framework does not invent something new for PORTILAME. It systematizes what was already there and makes it transferable to any organization.

---

This week was a journey. Five days. Ten professors. Five group members from five countries with five very different organizational realities.

We struggled with the scope — how do you compress this much depth into a seven-minute presentation? We debated what to include and what to leave out. We questioned whether our framework was original or just a repackaging of existing models — it is not. ANCHOR emerged organically from the intersection of programme content, real organizational data, and Appreciative Inquiry.

The losses: sleep. Comfort. The illusion that leadership can be reduced to a simple model.

The gains: a framework that actually works. Real evidence. Real tools. Real proof that when you stop fixing and start amplifying, everything changes.

---

An anchor doesn't fight the storm. It holds the ship steady so the crew can navigate.

That is holistic leadership. Not predicting the waves. Building a ship that stays steady.

Thank you for listening.
