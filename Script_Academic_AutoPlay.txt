[SLIDE 1 — TITLE]

Good afternoon. We are Group 3. Our challenge this week was simple to state and hard to answer: how can holistic leadership help organizations lead effectively in times of constant change?

We built our answer from real experience — not theory. Five people, five organizations, five countries. And one question: what actually works?

---

[SLIDE 2 — DISCOVER]

We started with the Appreciative Inquiry question: when was leadership at its best?

Across all five organizations, the answers converged around the same five truths.

Trust came first — always. Not trust as a policy, but trust as a daily choice. The best leaders earned it by extending it first.

Flexibility unlocked potential. When people had autonomy — especially during disruption — they found better solutions than any instruction manual could provide.

Open communication meant sharing optimism, acknowledging effort, and accepting imperfection. Not performing confidence — being transparent.

Proactive support structures were built in before crisis hit. The organizations that thrived were the ones where support already existed.

And shared purpose reduced uncertainty. When people understood the why, they navigated the how themselves.

The key insight: every one of these truths is about focusing on people's strengths and creating conditions for them to succeed — not fixing what's broken.

---

[SLIDE 3 — DREAM]

A child of an employee said this during a company event. And it stopped the room.

This is the dream. Not metrics. Not awards. Not efficiency ratios.

Children who see their parents come home fulfilled, proud, and inspired. That is what a truly healthy organization looks like from the outside.

The dream is organizations where people thrive — not just survive. Where leaders focus on amplifying each person's strengths, creating the conditions for people to be at their best. Where change is met with resilience, not fear.

As leaders, we must shift from fixing weaknesses to amplifying strengths. When you focus on what people do best and build around that, performance becomes sustainable — and human.

---

[SLIDE 4 — DESIGN]

From five days of sessions, science, and group reflection, we distilled six leadership practices.

Self-awareness — know your strengths, your biases, your energy patterns. Leading from your best self makes others follow with theirs.

Trust-building narrative — share the real story. Values, purpose, vulnerability. People follow leaders they believe, not leaders who perform.

Healthy rhythms — protect energy through cadence. Respect biological clocks, not just business clocks.

Psychological safety — create space for mistakes, for questions, for honest feedback. Belonging is not comfort — it is the safety to be real.

Growth through ownership — give people responsibility, not just tasks. Focus on each person's strengths and create conditions to amplify them.

Mindful resilience — build reflection into daily leadership. Pause before reacting. Seek the blind spots.

Our Day 4 group work reinforced these with three principles: healthy organizational rules, genuine employee wellbeing, and creating great environments for human energy.

---

[SLIDE 5 — DESTINY]

None of this matters unless it becomes action.

In the first thirty days: listen and diagnose. Run a simple well-being pulse. Find where trust, energy, and safety are strong — and where they need attention. Start from strengths, not from gaps.

In the first ninety days: introduce practices. Start small — weekly reflection rituals, peer recognition, protected recovery time. Build habits before building systems.

In the first year: embed and evolve. Integrate practices into culture. Measure impact through engagement and energy — not just performance numbers. Adjust based on real feedback.

The commitment is not perfection. It is starting. And starting from what is already working — amplifying strengths rather than fixing problems.

---

[SLIDE 6 — CLOSE]

Leading through change is not about having all the answers.

It is about creating the conditions for people to find them together. About focusing on what makes each person strong, and building around that.

Thank you.
